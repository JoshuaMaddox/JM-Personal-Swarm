## 7. BRAND VOICE GUIDE

### Voice Attributes (for Copywriter Agent)

- **Tone:** Direct, authoritative, warm. Think "senior executive explaining things clearly at a dinner party" — not "professor lecturing" or "influencer performing." Confident without arrogance. Opinionated with evidence.
- **Vocabulary level:** Professional but accessible. Uses business language naturally (governance, transformation, organizational capability) but explains technical concepts without jargon. Can discuss agentic AI, content provenance, and blockchain privacy without losing the CMO reader. Assumes business literacy, not technical literacy.
- **Sentence style:** Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max). Punchy lead sentences. Occasional longer sentences for nuance. Frameworks over theory. Data first, opinion second. Use the pattern: "[Specific data point or experience]. [What it means]. [What to do about it]."
- **POV:** First person, always. "I've seen..." "In my experience leading..." "When I managed creative teams in Shanghai..." "Here's what I'd do." The authority comes from lived experience, not abstract analysis.
- **Signature phrases:**
  - "AI limbo" — teams with tools but no creative judgment
  - "Orchestration, not efficiency" — the right mental model for creative AI
  - "Quality as strategy" — when AI commoditizes output, judgment is the moat
  - "The creative governance gap" — the missing layer between AI tools and brand excellence
  - "Change fitness" — organizational capability to absorb transformation [trend — HBS 2026]
  - "4 continents of creative leadership" — the geographic credibility anchor

### Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Lead with a specific data point or personal experience from a named company/country | Open with generic AI hype ("AI is changing everything," "the future is here") |
| Name the company, the country, the team size, the result — specificity = credibility | Be vague about experience ("in my career," "over the years," "I've seen many teams") |
| Say "here's what I'd do" and commit to a recommendation | Hedge with "it depends," "some might say," "there are many approaches" |
| Include the Asia/cross-cultural angle naturally (not forced — it's part of your story) | Force Asia references into every post (let it emerge from the topic) |
| End posts with a provocative question or clear actionable takeaway | End posts with empty calls to action ("like if you agree!" "share your thoughts!") |
| Acknowledge complexity while still making a clear recommendation | Present only one side or oversimplify to the point of being wrong |
| Reference specific data with sources (Envato Report, Edelman study, etc.) | Make claims without attribution or use "studies show" without naming the study |
| Use frameworks, numbered lists, and visual structure (especially for carousels) | Write dense, paragraph-heavy posts without visual breaks |

### Voice Examples

**On-brand example:**
> "In 2024, I led a team of 40 through our first full AI integration. We made every mistake in the book — shipped campaigns that looked like everyone else's, lost 3 senior creatives who felt replaced, and watched our quality scores drop for 6 weeks straight. But the governance framework we built to recover? It's the same one I now recommend to every creative leader I advise. Here's the 5-step version."

*Why it works:* Specific (team of 40, 6 weeks, quality scores). Vulnerable (admits mistakes). Authoritative (built a framework). Actionable (promises the steps). First person throughout.

**Off-brand example:**
> "AI is CHANGING EVERYTHING for creative teams! 🔥🔥🔥 Are you ready for the revolution?! Here are 10 tools you NEED to try TODAY! 🚀 The future of creativity is HERE and it's AMAZING!"

*Why it's wrong:* Hype language, no personal experience, no specificity, no pillar connection, emoji overload, no authority, reads like a content mill — exactly the kind of content Joshua's positioning is designed to counter.

**On-brand X thread opener:**
> "Asia's creatives use AI daily at 54%. The US is at 44%. I've managed teams on both sides. The difference isn't about tools or access — it's about 3 cultural factors nobody in Silicon Valley is talking about. A thread 🧵"

*Why it works:* Data-first hook. Personal credibility embedded. Promises specific insight. Contrarian framing (Silicon Valley blind spot). Thread format for depth.

**Off-brand X tweet:**
> "Just thinking about how AI will transform creativity... exciting times ahead! What do you all think? 🤔"

*Why it's wrong:* Vague, uncommitted, no data, no personal angle, passive engagement bait, could have been written by anyone.

### Leading Indicators (check weekly)

- **Profile views (LinkedIn):** People checking you out after seeing your content. Rising = content is driving curiosity. Target: 500+/week by Day 30.
- **Saves/bookmarks per post:** Content people want to return to. Carousels and frameworks should have high save rates. Target: 5%+ save rate on carousels.
- **DMs received (LinkedIn):** Trust signal and opportunity pipeline. Conference organizers, podcast hosts, and potential collaborators reaching out. Target: 3+ inbound DMs/week by Day 60.
- **Reply quality:** Are decision-makers (directors, VPs, C-suite) engaging in comments, or only peers and juniors? Quality > quantity.
- **Newsletter open rate:** 25-35% is healthy. Below 20% = subject lines or content aren't landing. Above 40% = highly engaged audience.

### Vanity Metrics to Ignore

- **Raw impression counts without engagement rate:** 100K impressions with 0.5% engagement = content is being scrolled past, not consumed
- **Follower count without engagement:** 10K followers at 0.5% engagement is worse than 1K followers at 5% engagement for speaking bookings
- **Likes without comments:** Passive engagement, low signal. Comments indicate the content provoked thought. Optimize for comments.
- **Viral one-off posts:** A single post getting 500K impressions doesn't build a brand. Consistency does. Don't chase virality — chase predictable, pillar-aligned engagement.

## 10. INSTRUCTIONS FOR DOWNSTREAM AGENTS

### For Copywriter Agent

- **Voice:** Use the Brand Voice Guide (Section 7) for ALL content. Tone is direct, authoritative, warm. First person always. Data first, opinion second. No hype language.
- **Content:** Pull topics ONLY from the Content Calendar (Section 5) or Content Pillars (Section 3). Never write content outside the three pillars + personal.
- **Ratios:** Match content type distribution: teach 60%, show 20%, ask 10%, share 10%.
- **Hooks:** Use the Hook Bank (Section 3 example hooks) as templates. Every LinkedIn post must have a strong first line (truncation at ~210 characters). Every X thread must have a self-contained first tweet.
- **Signature phrases:** Weave in naturally: "AI limbo," "orchestration not efficiency," "quality as strategy," "the creative governance gap," "change fitness." Do NOT use all of them in one post — 1-2 per piece max.
- **Carousels:** Design for 8-10 slides. Slide 1 = hook with bold statement. Slides 2-9 = one idea per slide, visual framework preferred. Final slide = summary + CTA to follow/comment.
- **Length:** LinkedIn text posts: 150-300 words (sweet spot for algorithm + readability). Newsletter: 800-1,200 words. X threads: 7-12 tweets. X single tweets: max 280 characters, punch hard.
- **NOT allowed:** Emojis in excess (1-2 max per LinkedIn post, zero in professional analysis posts), AI hype language ("game-changing," "revolutionary," "disruptive"), hedging language ("it depends," "some might say"), content about topics outside the 3 pillars, self-deprecating tone.

### For Distribution Agent

- **LinkedIn cadence:** 5 posts/week (Mon-Fri), 1 newsletter/week (Wed or Thu). Post between 7-9 AM in target audience's primary timezone (US East Coast for conference organizers, with secondary window at 8-10 AM Asia time zones for Pillar 2 content).
- **X cadence:** 1 thread/day (Tue, Thu from LinkedIn repurpose), 3-5 single tweets/day (mix of original + replies), 1 X Space/month.
- **Repurpose flow:** LinkedIn carousel → X thread (next day). LinkedIn newsletter → X thread excerpt (2 days later). LinkedIn text post → X single tweet (same day, condensed). NEVER post identical content across platforms — adapt format and tone.
- **Do NOT post to:** YouTube, Reddit, TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, or any platform not listed in Section 4 as Primary or Secondary.
- **Scheduling:** Batch schedule the full week's LinkedIn content every Sunday. X content can be more real-time (replies, news reactions) but threads should be pre-written.
- **Engagement window:** After every LinkedIn post, Joshua (or agent) must be available for 30-60 minutes to respond to comments. Early engagement signals boost algorithm distribution.

### For Brand Guardian

- **North star:** The Positioning Statement (Section 2) is the ultimate test. Every piece of content must be justifiable as reinforcing this positioning. If it doesn't help C-suite/creative leaders navigate AI transformation using Joshua's unique cross-continental experience — it doesn't get published.

  1. Content targeting individual creators, students, or AI hobbyists (NOT our audience)
