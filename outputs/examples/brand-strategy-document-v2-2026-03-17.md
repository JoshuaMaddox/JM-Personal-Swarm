- **Key subtopics:**
  1. "I'm building X with AI — here's how" (live build documentation)
  2. Vibe coding walkthroughs for non-technical builders
  3. AI agent workflows — building with Claude Code, Cursor, MCP servers

- **Your edge:** 18 years of shipping creative products + current daily AI building experience. You can teach judgment, not just skills.
- **Key subtopics:**

  2. How to scope an AI project so you actually finish it
  3. Quality control: making sure AI output doesn't suck
  4. The AI builder's stack — what I actually use daily and why
  5. Vibe coding guardrails — how to build fast without building fragile
  6. When AI is the wrong tool (and what to use instead)
  7. From "I use ChatGPT for emails" to "I build products with AI" — the progression
  8. The creative advantage: why design thinking makes you a better AI builder
- **Example hooks:**
  1. "Everyone asks 'which AI tool should I use?' Wrong question. Here are the 3 questions you should ask instead:"
  2. "I've built 12 AI projects this year. 8 failed. Here are the 3 patterns that separate the winners:"

### Pillar 3: Experiments & Outcomes (25% of content)

- **Core topic:** The lab reports. What happened when we tried X at Yaru or in personal experiments. Real data, real results, honest assessment. This is the "proof of work" pillar — showing that you don't just talk about building, you have receipts.

  2. AI tool head-to-head comparisons with real projects (not synthetic benchmarks)

  5. "The experiment that changed how I think about AI building"

  8. Failed experiments: what I learned and what I'd do differently
- **Example hooks:**
  1. "We ran an experiment at Yaru: can a non-technical founder build and launch an AI product in 6 weeks? Here are the results:"
  2. "I tested 5 AI coding tools on the same project. The winner surprised me. Full breakdown:"

## 7. BRAND VOICE GUIDE

### Voice Attributes (for Copywriter Agent)

- **Tone:** Energetic, curious, direct. Think "the friend who's always building something cool and can't wait to show you how." Not academic. Not corporate. Not hype-bro. Enthusiastic about the WORK, not about the CLOUT.
- **Vocabulary level:** Accessible but not dumbed down. Uses "vibe coding," "ship," "MVP," "stack" naturally. Explains technical concepts through analogy and example, not jargon. Can talk about MCP servers and agent workflows without losing the designer who's learning to code.
- **Sentence style:** Short, punchy, action-oriented. "Here's what I built." "Here's what happened." "Here's what I learned." Numbered lists and frameworks for educational content. Stories for personal content. Data points as proof — "40 hours saved," "built in 4 hours," "3 tools, 1 weekend, 1 shipped product."
- **POV:** First person, always. "I built..." "I tested..." "Here's what I'm working on..." "Watch me build this." The authority comes from DOING, not from knowing.
- **Signature phrases:**
  - "Build with me" — the invitation to learn by watching and participating
  - "Ship it" — the builder's mantra, borrowed from the culture (Pieter Levels: "ship more")
  - "Here's what actually happened" — the anti-hype, honest-results framing
  - "From Bangkok" / "Building from Bangkok" — the geographic anchor
  - "The creative builder's advantage" — why creative background + AI building = superpower
  - "Idea to product" — the transformation promise

### Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Show your screen. Show the tools. Show the actual build process. | Talk about building without showing it |
| Name specific tools, specific timelines, specific outcomes | Be vague ("I used AI to build something cool") |
| Share what broke and how you fixed it | Only share victories |
| Invite people to build along with you | Lecture from above |
| Credit the community (tag people who inspired ideas) | Act like you invented everything |
| Say "I don't know yet, but I'm going to find out" | Pretend to be an expert on everything |
| Use data: hours saved, tools used, iterations needed | Make claims without receipts |
| Be excited about the WORK and the PROCESS | Be excited about follower counts or engagement metrics |

### Voice Examples

**On-brand example (LinkedIn):**
> "I built a complete customer onboarding flow with Claude Code in 4 hours yesterday. A year ago this would've been a 2-week sprint with a dev team. Here's the thing though — it broke twice. The first time was my fault (bad prompt). The second time was fascinating: the AI made a design decision I never would have. I kept it. Here's the 8-step process I used, including both failures:"

*Why it works:* Specific (4 hours, 2 failures). Shows the process. Admits mistakes. Finds the interesting moment. Promises the framework. First person throughout.

**Off-brand example:**
> "AI is absolutely REVOLUTIONIZING how we build products! 🚀🔥 The future is HERE and every entrepreneur needs to get on board NOW or get left behind! Here are 10 AI tools you NEED to try TODAY!"

*Why it's wrong:* Hype language, no personal experience, no specific build, no process, fear-mongering, tool list format (Matt Wolfe's lane), reads like AI-generated content.

**On-brand X thread opener:**
> "I just spent 6 hours building the same product in Cursor and Claude Code. Same spec. Same starting point. Different results. Here's every decision point, and the clear winner 🧵"

**Off-brand X tweet:**
> "So excited about the future of AI! What tools are you guys using? Drop them below! 👇"

*Why it's wrong:* Generic, no personal experience, engagement bait without value, could be anyone.

**On-brand YouTube Short script:**
> "Watch me build a complete landing page with AI in 90 seconds. Starting with just a one-line description. [screen recording of actual build] That's it. Took 3 minutes in real time. The tool is [name]. Link to the full walkthrough in my LinkedIn."

*Why it works:* Visual proof. Specific outcome. Named tool. Cross-platform reference. Under 60 seconds.

### Leading Indicators (check weekly)

- **Saves/bookmarks per post** — content people want to return to. Carousels should have 5%+ save rate. This is the #1 algorithm signal on LinkedIn in 2026.
- **Profile views** — people checking you out after seeing content. Rising = content is driving curiosity. Target: 1,000+/week by Day 30.
- **Reply quality on X** — are other builders engaging? Are accounts with 5K+ followers replying? Quality > quantity.

### Vanity Metrics to Ignore

- **Raw impressions without engagement** — scrolled past, not consumed
- **Likes without saves/comments** — passive, low signal. Optimize for saves (LinkedIn) and replies (X).
- **Follower count without engagement rate** — 10K followers at 0.5% engagement is worse than 3K at 5%
- **Individual viral posts** — don't chase virality. Build the machine. Virality is a bonus, not a strategy.
