# Brand Guardian Review Checklist

Rapid-fire reference for content reviews. Use alongside the full SKILL.md workflow.

---

## Fast Review Path

Five questions that catch 80% of issues. Start here for quick reviews:

1. **Does the first line match the voice?** Direct, authoritative, first person, specific. If the opening is passive, generic, or could be anyone — stop and flag.
2. **Could a competitor have written this?** If yes, positioning is too generic. The content must reflect executive leadership across 4 continents, not general AI commentary.
3. **Is there at least one named company, country, or data point?** Specificity is the brand's signature. Vague generalizations fail the voice test.
4. **Does it fit in exactly one pillar?** If you can't immediately say "this is Pillar 2" or "this is Personal," pillar compliance fails. Ambiguity means the angle isn't sharp enough.
5. **Would you be embarrassed if a conference organizer read this?** This is the quality bar. If it feels lightweight, derivative, or self-promotional without substance — reject.

---

## Check 1: Positioning Alignment

**What to look for:**
- Executive perspective (leading teams, making org-level decisions, board-level framing)
- Multi-continent or cross-cultural reference (earned through experience, not tourism)
- Authority signal (speaking from having done it, not having read about it)

**Common failures:**
- Generic AI commentary that any LinkedIn user could post. Severity: BLOCKING.
- Positioning as a learner or explorer rather than a leader. Severity: BLOCKING.

---

## Check 2: Audience Targeting

**What to look for:**
- Language that resonates with C-suite (strategic framing, business impact, organizational scale)
- Problems that only executives face (governance, cross-team alignment, board reporting)
- Conference-organizer appeal (unique angle, speaker-worthy insight)

**Common failures:**
- Tutorial-style content aimed at individual practitioners ("here's how to prompt better"). Severity: BLOCKING.
- Beginner-friendly framing that signals "intro level" ("if you're new to AI..."). Severity: BLOCKING.

---

## Check 3: Pillar Compliance

**What to look for:**
- Clear mapping to exactly one pillar (AI Creative Governance, Global Creative AI Divide, Creative Authenticity, or Personal)
- Personal content still connects to a pillar indirectly
- Pillar angle is specific, not just a topic match

**Common failures:**
- Content that's broadly "about AI" without a pillar-specific angle. Severity: BLOCKING.
- Personal content that's pure lifestyle with zero professional connection. Severity: WARNING if rare, BLOCKING if pattern.

---

## Check 4: Voice Compliance

**What to look for:**
- First person POV ("I" not "we" or passive voice)
- Direct, authoritative tone with warmth (not cold, not casual)
- Specific examples over abstractions (named companies, countries, results)

**Common failures:**
- Passive voice throughout ("It has been observed that AI is changing..."). Severity: WARNING.
- Corporate press release tone ("We are pleased to announce..."). Severity: WARNING.

---

## Check 5: Content Type Ratio

**What to look for:**
- Teach content actually teaches (framework, methodology, insight)
- Show content has a concrete example or case study
- Weekly mix tracking toward 60/20/10/10

**Common failures:**
- Mislabeled content type — "teach" that's actually "share" (just linking to someone else's work). Severity: WARNING.
- Batch reviews showing 100% teach content with no show/ask/share variety. Severity: WARNING.

---

## Check 6: Format Compliance

**What to look for:**
- Word count within spec (text post: 150-300, newsletter: 800-1200)
- Slide count within spec (carousel: 8-10)
- Thread length within spec (7-12 tweets, each under 280 chars)

**Common failures:**
- Text posts running 400+ words (should be a newsletter or carousel instead). Severity: WARNING.
- Threads with tweets over 280 characters (will be truncated on X). Severity: BLOCKING.

---

## Check 7: Red Flag Scan

**What to look for:**
- Blocklisted hype words: "game-changing," "revolutionary," "disruptive," "transformative," "paradigm shift"
- Hedging without commitment: "it depends," "some might say," "there are pros and cons"
- Emoji count (max 2 per post)

**Common failures:**
- Opening with "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape..." — hype-adjacent and generic. Severity: BLOCKING.
- Concluding with "What do you think?" after presenting zero opinion. Severity: WARNING (hedging).

---

## Severity Quick Reference

| Level | Impact | Action Required |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| BLOCKING | Causes REJECTED verdict | Must fix before publication |
| WARNING | Causes APPROVED WITH EDITS | Should fix, specific edits provided |
| SUGGESTION | No impact on verdict | Optional improvement, noted for consideration |
