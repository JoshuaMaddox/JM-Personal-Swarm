# Positioning Drift Detection

How to identify, measure, and correct positioning drift before it damages brand equity.

---

## What Positioning Drift Looks Like

Drift is rarely a single bad post. It's a gradual slide that compounds over weeks. Watch for these symptoms:

**Audience confusion signals:**
- Comments from people outside the target audience (individual creators asking for tool recommendations, students asking for beginner advice)
- Engagement spikes on off-pillar content that attract the wrong followers
- DMs or inquiries that don't match the brand's service offering

**Engagement pattern shifts:**
- Core pillar content starts underperforming relative to its historical baseline
- Off-pillar or generic content gets more engagement, creating a temptation loop
- Conference organizers and C-suite connections engage less frequently

**Content quality signals:**
- Posts become easier to write (a sign they're getting more generic)
- Competitor content starts looking interchangeable with yours
- "Anyone could have written this" becomes true more often than not

## Drift Scoring

Track posts per week that map cleanly to a pillar. "Cleanly" means you can identify the pillar in the first two sentences without guessing.

| Weekly Score | Status | Action |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| 80-100% on-pillar | Green — on track | No action needed |
| 60-79% on-pillar | Yellow alert | Review the off-pillar posts. Were they intentional (personal content) or accidental drift? |
| Below 60% on-pillar | Red alert | Pause content production. Run recovery protocol immediately. |

**How to count:**
- Posts that map to Pillar 1, 2, or 3 = on-pillar
- Personal posts that indirectly reinforce a pillar = on-pillar
- Personal posts with zero pillar connection = off-pillar (acceptable at 10% max)
- Posts that don't map to any pillar or personal = off-pillar (never acceptable)

## Common Drift Triggers

### 1. Trending Topic Temptation
A major AI news story breaks and the instinct is to comment. If the story doesn't connect to one of the 3 pillars, the post is off-pillar regardless of how timely it feels.

**Guard rail:** Before posting on trending news, answer: "Which pillar does this serve?" If the answer requires more than 5 seconds of thought, don't post.

### 2. Company News Without Pillar Connection
COTI makes an announcement. The instinct is to amplify. But pure company promotion without a pillar angle is off-brand.

**Guard rail:** Every COTI-related post must teach something about AI Creative Governance, the Global Creative AI Divide, or Creative Authenticity. The company news is the hook, not the point.

### 3. Breadth Over Depth
The desire to appear well-rounded leads to posting about adjacent topics: AI ethics broadly, productivity tools, leadership generalities, tech industry commentary.

**Guard rail:** Depth is the brand. "The world's foremost voice on AI creative governance" beats "someone who has interesting thoughts about lots of AI topics."

### 4. Responding to Competitors Outside Your Lane
A competitor posts about AI in education. The temptation is to counter-post. But if AI in education isn't in your pillars, the response is off-brand.

**Guard rail:** Only engage competitor content that overlaps with your 3 pillars. Let them own their lane. Own yours.

## Recovery Protocol

When drift is detected (2+ off-pillar posts in a week, or yellow/red alert on drift scoring):

### Step 1: Immediate Correction (Days 1-2)
Post 2 strong on-pillar pieces. Choose your strongest pillar (Pillar 1: AI Creative Governance, at 40%). Make them teach-type content — frameworks, methodologies, specific insights. These posts should be unmistakably on-brand.

### Step 2: Root Cause Analysis (Day 3)
Review what caused the drift:
- Was it reactive posting (responding to news/trends without filtering through pillars)?
- Was it audience pressure (engagement rewards for off-pillar content)?
- Was it creative fatigue (pillars felt stale, so you wandered)?
- Was it strategic (testing a new direction intentionally)?

### Step 3: Decision Point (Day 3-4)
If drift was accidental: resume normal content calendar with renewed pillar discipline.

If drift was audience-driven (they clearly wanted the off-pillar content): consider whether a pillar needs updating. This is a brand-strategy decision — flag it for a strategy refresh rather than adapting content unilaterally.

If drift was creative fatigue: find a new angle within existing pillars rather than expanding scope. Depth within pillars is infinite — drill down, don't spread out.

## The NOT-for List as a Hard Boundary

These 5 excluded audiences are non-negotiable. Content that targets them is always a blocking failure.

### 1. Individual Creators
Content that would attract solo freelancers, independent artists, or individual content creators.
- **Example violation:** "Top 10 Midjourney prompts for better creative output"
- **Why it fails:** Targets practitioners, not executives. No governance angle, no organizational lens.

### 2. AI Engineers
Content that would attract technical practitioners building or fine-tuning AI systems.
- **Example violation:** "How to fine-tune LLMs for creative tasks"
- **Why it fails:** Technical implementation content. The brand advises leaders who manage teams that do this, not the engineers doing it.

### 3. Crypto/Web3 Traders
Content that would attract people interested in token economics, trading, or speculative investment.
- **Example violation:** "COTI token analysis Q1 2026 — price outlook and staking strategy"
- **Why it fails:** Pure financial content with no connection to creative AI governance or any pillar.

### 4. Small Business Owners
Content that would attract solopreneurs, small agencies, or bootstrapped startups looking for tools.
- **Example violation:** "AI tools for solopreneurs: automate your creative workflow on a budget"
- **Why it fails:** Wrong scale. The brand speaks to organizations with creative teams, not individuals managing their own output.

### 5. Students and Beginners
Content that would attract people new to AI, new to creative industries, or learning fundamentals.
- **Example violation:** "Getting started with AI creativity: a beginner's guide"
- **Why it fails:** The brand assumes executive-level knowledge. Beginner content undermines authority positioning.

## Drift Detection in Batch Reviews

When reviewing a week's content in batch mode, calculate drift score automatically:

1. Count total posts
2. Count posts that map cleanly to a pillar (including personal-with-pillar-connection)
3. Calculate percentage: on-pillar posts / total posts
4. Apply scoring table from above
5. If yellow or red alert: include drift alert in the weekly dashboard and recommend recovery protocol
