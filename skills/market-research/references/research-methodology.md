# Research Methodology Reference

## Track A: Audience Intelligence - Detailed Methods

### Pain Point Discovery Process
1. Search Reddit for "[niche] + struggling with" OR "help me with" OR "can't figure out"
2. Search X for "[niche] + frustrated" OR "wish someone would" OR "anyone know how to"
3. Check Quora/Stack Exchange for most-asked questions in the niche
4. Look at Amazon reviews of books in the niche (1-star and 5-star reveal pain points)
5. Check YouTube comments on top videos in the niche (sort by newest)

### Language Mining
Extract exact phrases the audience uses. Do NOT paraphrase into marketing-speak.
- Wrong: "Users experience difficulties with workflow optimization"
- Right: "I keep losing hours trying to figure out which tool to use" (r/productivity, 342 upvotes)

### Audience Watering Holes Checklist
- Subreddits (search reddit.com/subreddits for niche terms)
- X/Twitter lists and communities
- Discord servers (search Disboard.org)
- Slack communities (search Slofile.com)
- Facebook Groups
- Newsletter communities (search Substack for niche)
- Podcast audiences (check Apple Podcasts categories)
- Conference/event communities

## Track B: Competitor Analysis - Detailed Methods

### Finding Competitors
1. Search X for top accounts posting about [niche] (sort by engagement)
2. Search LinkedIn for "[niche] + thought leader" OR "creator"
3. Search YouTube for top channels in the niche
4. Search Substack for newsletters in the niche
5. Check SparkToro or similar for audience overlap data
6. Ask: "Who does the target audience already follow?"

### Per-Competitor Data Collection Template
```
Competitor: @handle
Platforms: [list]
Follower Counts: [per platform]
Posting Frequency: [per platform, per week]
Content Mix: [% threads, % short posts, % long-form, % video, % carousels]
Top 3 Posts (last 30 days): [links + engagement metrics]
Brand Positioning Statement: [1 sentence, what they claim to be]
Audience Overlap: [how much their audience overlaps with target]
Strengths: [what they do well]
Weaknesses: [gaps in their content or positioning]
```

### Engagement Rate Calculation
- X: (likes + retweets + replies + bookmarks) / followers * 100
- LinkedIn: (likes + comments + reposts) / followers * 100
- Instagram: (likes + comments + saves) / followers * 100
- YouTube: (views in first 48h) / subscribers * 100

Benchmarks:
- X: 1-3% is average, 3-6% is good, 6%+ is excellent
- LinkedIn: 2-4% is average, 4-8% is good, 8%+ is excellent
- Newsletter: 20-30% open rate is average, 30-45% is good, 45%+ is excellent

## Track C: Trend Identification - Detailed Methods

### Trend Velocity Classification
- **Rising:** Mentions increasing week-over-week, not yet mainstream
- **Peaking:** Maximum engagement and attention, high saturation
- **Declining:** Engagement dropping, audience fatigue setting in
- **Cyclical:** Returns periodically (seasonal, event-driven)
- **Evergreen:** Consistent demand regardless of timing

### Signal Sources (ranked by leading-indicator strength)
1. Reddit new posts gaining traction (earliest signal)
2. X trending topics and hashtag velocity
3. Google Trends (lagging but broad)
4. Newsletter topic frequency (curated signal)
5. Conference talk topics (6-month leading indicator)
6. VC investment themes (12-month leading indicator)

### Saturation Assessment
- Low: Fewer than 5 notable creators covering this regularly
- Medium: 5-15 creators, but differentiation still possible
- High: 15+ creators, would need unique angle to stand out
- Oversaturated: Audience actively complaining about too much content on this topic

## Track D: Platform Opportunity Mapping - Detailed Methods

### Platform Selection Criteria
Score each platform 1-5 on these dimensions:
1. Is the target audience active here? (audience presence)
2. Is there a gap in quality content for this niche? (content gap)
3. Does the algorithm favor new creators? (growth potential)
4. Does the content format match the brand's strengths? (format fit)
5. Can content here be repurposed elsewhere? (leverage potential)

Total score /25. Platforms scoring 18+ are primary targets.

## Track E: Content Gap Analysis - Detailed Methods

### Gap Identification Process
1. List top 20 topics competitors cover
2. List top 20 questions the audience asks
3. Cross-reference: which audience questions have NO or POOR creator answers?
4. Those are your gaps

### Gap Quality Assessment
Not all gaps are worth filling. Score each gap:
- **Audience demand:** How many people are asking about this? (1-5)
- **Supply quality:** How bad is the existing content? (1-5, where 5 = terrible)
- **Brand fit:** Can this brand credibly cover this topic? (1-5)
- **Effort:** How hard is it to create good content on this? (1-5, where 5 = easy)

Total score /20. Gaps scoring 14+ are high-priority.
