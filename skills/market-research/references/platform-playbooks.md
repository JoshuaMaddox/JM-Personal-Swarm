# Platform Research Playbooks

## X / Twitter

### Research Approach
- Search niche keywords using X's search with date filters
- Check "Top" tab for highest-engagement content, "Latest" for trend velocity
- Analyze reply threads on popular posts (the real conversations happen here)
- Bookmarks are a stronger signal than likes (people save what they'll use)
- Check X Spaces in the niche for recurring topics and active community members

### Algorithm Signals (Current as of Q1 2026)
- Long-form posts (240+ chars) outperform short posts
- Threads with 4-8 posts hit the sweet spot
- Images and video increase reach 30-50%
- Replies in first hour are the strongest ranking signal
- Posting between 7-9 AM and 5-7 PM user's timezone performs best

### Red Flags for Research
- Accounts with high followers but low engagement (purchased followers)
- Engagement pods (same accounts always replying)
- Retweet-heavy metrics without genuine replies

## LinkedIn

### Research Approach
- Search niche keywords, filter by "Posts" and "This week"
- Check who's in the "Top Voices" for relevant topics
- Analyze comment sections (LinkedIn comments are often higher quality than posts)
- Look at "Featured" sections of competitor profiles for their best content
- Check company pages in the niche for content themes

### Algorithm Signals (Current as of Q1 2026)
- Text-only posts and document carousels outperform link posts
- Posts that generate comments in first 90 minutes get amplified
- Personal stories with professional lessons have highest engagement
- Posting Tuesday-Thursday 8-10 AM performs best
- Tagging more than 3 people reduces reach

### Research-Specific Notes
- LinkedIn engagement rates look lower but conversion rates are higher
- 1 LinkedIn impression is worth roughly 5-10 X impressions for B2B
- Check "Articles" separately from posts (different algorithm, different audience)

## Reddit

### Research Approach
- Identify 5-10 subreddits where the target audience participates
- Sort by "Top" (past month) for proven interest topics
- Sort by "New" + rising upvotes for emerging trends
- Read comment threads deeply - the real pain points surface in comments
- Search "[niche] + recommendation" for what the audience already trusts
- Check moderator rules for content restrictions (important for future strategy)

### Research-Specific Notes
- Reddit users are the most honest audience source (anonymity = candor)
- Upvote counts on questions indicate audience demand more accurately than any other platform
- The language used in Reddit posts is the most authentic audience voice available
- Search both post titles AND comments (different insights in each)
- Weekly/monthly recurring threads often contain concentrated insight

### Red Flags for Research
- Subreddits with heavy self-promotion (low signal quality)
- Very small subreddits (<5K members) may not be representative
- Check if subreddit has active moderation (inactive = lower quality)

## Blog / SEO

### Research Approach
- Search niche keywords, assess what's ranking on page 1
- Check "People Also Ask" boxes for content gap signals
- Use Google Trends to compare topic interest over time
- Check competitor blogs for: posting frequency, top categories, content depth
- Look for thin content ranking well (opportunity to create something better)

### Metrics to Capture
- Domain authority of competing blogs (rough proxy for difficulty)
- Content freshness of ranking pages (old content = opportunity to update)
- Word count of ranking content (indicator of depth expectations)
- Featured snippet presence (opportunity for authoritative answers)

## YouTube

### Research Approach
- Search niche keywords, sort by "This month" for trends
- Check view-to-subscriber ratios (high ratio = topic has broad appeal)
- Read comments for audience questions and pain points
- Check video descriptions for related topics and keywords
- Look at "Suggested" videos alongside top performers

### Research-Specific Notes
- YouTube is both a platform and a search engine (SEO applies)
- Long-form educational content has longer shelf life
- Shorts are growing but have different audience dynamics
- Check if competitors use YouTube or if it's a wide-open gap

## Newsletter / Email

### Research Approach
- Search Substack, Beehiiv, ConvertKit for newsletters in the niche
- Check subscriber counts where visible
- Subscribe to top 3 competitor newsletters for content analysis
- Look for sponsorship patterns (indicates monetization potential)
- Check open rate benchmarks for the niche

### Research-Specific Notes
- Newsletter is the only owned-media channel (not algorithm-dependent)
- High subscriber loyalty but slow growth
- Best for nurturing an existing audience, not for discovery
