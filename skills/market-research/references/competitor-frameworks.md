# Competitor Analysis Frameworks Reference

## Positioning Map Framework

### Two-Axis Positioning
Choose the two dimensions most relevant to the niche and map competitors:

Common axis pairs for personal brands:
- **Tactical vs. Strategic** (x-axis) x **Beginner vs. Advanced** (y-axis)
- **Technical vs. Accessible** x **Broad vs. Niche**
- **Data-Driven vs. Story-Driven** x **Enterprise vs. Individual**
- **Free Content vs. Premium** x **Short-Form vs. Long-Form**

### Positioning Quadrant Analysis
```
                    Advanced
                       |
           [Gap?]      |     [Saturated?]
                       |
   Tactical -----------+----------- Strategic
                       |
           [Crowded?]  |     [Gap?]
                       |
                    Beginner
```

For each quadrant: count the number of competitors, assess content quality,
identify if the user has credibility to occupy that position.

## Content Differentiation Matrix

### The 4 Dimensions of Content Differentiation
1. **Topic:** What you talk about
2. **Format:** How you present it (thread, video, carousel, essay)
3. **Angle:** Your unique perspective or framework
4. **Depth:** Surface-level vs. deep-dive

A creator only needs to be differentiated on 1-2 dimensions to stand out.

### Assessment Template
```
Competitor: [name]
Topic Uniqueness:     [1-5] - Do they cover topics no one else does?
Format Innovation:    [1-5] - Do they use formats others don't?
Angle Originality:    [1-5] - Do they have a unique POV?
Depth Commitment:     [1-5] - Do they go deeper than others?
Overall Differentiation Score: [X/20]
```

Competitors scoring below 10/20 are vulnerable to displacement.

## Authority Indicator Framework

### Signals of Established Authority
- Published book in the niche
- Speaking at major conferences
- Quoted by mainstream media
- Large email list (proxy: course/product sales volume)
- Longevity (3+ years consistent content)
- Celebrity endorsements or co-creations

### Signals of Rising Authority (easier to compete with)
- Growing fast but less than 2 years in the space
- No book or major media presence yet
- Authority built primarily on one platform
- Small but engaged community
- Original frameworks or coined terms gaining adoption

## SWOT Template for Competitor Analysis

For each top-3 competitor:

```
COMPETITOR: [name]
STRENGTHS:
- [what they do best]
- [unique advantage they have]

WEAKNESSES:
- [content gaps]
- [platform gaps]
- [audience complaints]
- [inconsistency areas]

OPPORTUNITIES (for our brand):
- [topics they ignore]
- [formats they don't use]
- [audiences they don't serve]

THREATS (if they improve):
- [what would make them harder to compete with]
```
