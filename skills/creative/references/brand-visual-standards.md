# Brand Visual Standards — Joshua Bryan Maddox

## 7-Point Brand Consistency Checklist

Run this check on every generated asset before delivery. Each check is PASS or FAIL. Any FAIL = regenerate.

---

### Check 1: Color Adherence

**PASS criteria:**
- Primary navy `#0A0F1E` or deep charcoal present as dominant dark tone
- Electric blue `#2563EB` present as accent (city lights, monitor glow, desk lamp LED)
- Warm amber `#D97706` present in foreground light sources (desk lamp, golden hour)
- No unintended colors dominant (no AI-typical magenta, no oversaturated teal)
- Background stays in the deep navy / charcoal / warm-amber / electric-blue family

**FAIL examples:**
- Image dominated by bright white or cream tones (wrong brand palette)
- Green cast across the scene (wrong color temperature)
- Overly warm orange throughout with no cool contrast (missing blue accent)
- Pastel tones anywhere (never pastel — always rich and saturated-but-natural)

---

### Check 2: Lighting Direction Consistency

**PASS criteria:**
- Warm practical light (amber desk lamp) comes from a consistent direction across the series
- Cool ambient light (city blue, window) comes from the window side
- Shadows fall consistently — if key light is camera-left in image 1, it stays camera-left
- Lighting feels motivated — you can identify a physical source for every lit surface

**FAIL examples:**
- Subject lit from directly above (AI default — produces flat, boring top-light)
- Perfectly even lighting with no shadows (studio-backdrop look — kills authenticity)
- Shadow direction changes between images in the same series
- Floating light with no identifiable source

---

### Check 3: Composition

**PASS criteria:**
- Rule of thirds: subject occupies one third of the frame, not dead center
- Negative space is intentional and consistent with the platform's text overlay needs
- Slight depth: foreground element + midground subject + background (city/window)
- No symmetrical left-right composition unless intentionally architectural

**FAIL examples:**
- Subject dead center, equal negative space on both sides (stock photo composition)
- Horizon line through the exact middle of the frame
- Everything at the same focal depth (no foreground/background separation)
- Subject cut off awkwardly at the frame edge

---

### Check 4: No AI Artifacts

**PASS criteria:**
- Hands (if present): correct number of fingers, anatomically plausible joints
- Text (if present): actually legible, not garbled letterforms
- Architecture: windows are rectangular, walls meet at 90 degrees
- Reflections: match the described environment, not generic blurs
- Shadows: attached to the objects that cast them, correct direction

**FAIL examples:**
- Extra or missing fingers
- Garbled text or pseudo-letters on screens/notebooks
- Windows with non-Euclidean geometry
- Shadows floating disconnected from objects
- Background architecture that warps or melts at the edges

---

### Check 5: Subject Framing (when Joshua appears)

**PASS criteria:**
- Three-quarter angle (not straight-on)
- Expression is active — mid-thought, concentrating, in motion — not posed
- Weight distributed naturally — not standing at rigid attention
- Eyes directed off-camera or at a meaningful point in the scene
- No direct-to-camera "business portrait" pose

**FAIL examples:**
- Dead-center, facing straight at camera, neutral expression (passport photo)
- Forced smile, artificial confidence pose
- Hands clasped in front in "ready to do business" stance
- Overly polished skin, poreless complexion (AI beauty default)
- Eyes that look painted rather than three-dimensional

---

### Check 6: Text-Safe Zones

**PASS criteria (by format):**
- **LinkedIn Post Header (1200×628):** Left third clear for text overlay OR image dark enough that white text reads over any zone
- **Carousel Slide (1080×1080):** Bottom 40% of frame intentionally dark/clear for copy
- **YouTube Thumbnail (1080×1920):** Upper 50% for face/focal subject, lower 40% clear for title text
- **X Post Image (1200×675):** Center 80% width available, top/bottom 20% can have visual interest

**FAIL examples:**
- Carousel slide with bright, busy pattern in the text zone (text would be unreadable)
- Thumbnail with face positioned in lower 30% (face will be covered by YouTube title)
- LinkedIn header with subject dead center in a complex background (no text-safe zone)

---

### Check 7: Authenticity Test

Three questions. All three must be YES for PASS.

1. **"Would someone ask who took this photo?"** — Does it look like it was taken by a photographer, not generated by an AI?
2. **"Would you find this exact composition on a stock photo site?"** — If YES, it fails. Stock photo framing = automatic FAIL.
3. **"Is there one specific detail in this image that proves it wasn't generated from a generic prompt?"** — A worn notebook spine, a specific city light pattern, steam rising at a particular angle. Generic = FAIL.

## Series Consistency Rules

When generating multiple assets for the same content batch:

1. **Same camera:** Sony A7 IV in every prompt. Do not vary.
2. **Same lens:** 35mm f/1.8. Vary aperture (`f/1.8` for shallow DOF, `f/4` for deeper) — but always 35mm.
3. **Same film stock:** Kodak Portra 400 for daylight/golden hour. Cinestill 800T for night/neon only.
4. **Same light direction:** If warm light comes from camera-right in image 1, it stays camera-right throughout the series.
5. **Same color temperature:** Don't mix cool-dominant and warm-dominant images in the same series.
6. **Change only one variable at a time** when iterating — if the composition isn't working, fix composition. Don't also change the lighting and the subject simultaneously.
