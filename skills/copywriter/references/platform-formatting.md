# Platform Formatting Rules

Platform-specific formatting rules for LinkedIn and X. Apply these to every
piece of content before delivery.

---

## LinkedIn Formatting

**Emoji usage:**
- 1-2 max per post.
- None in analysis or data-heavy posts.
- Acceptable in ask and share posts.
- Never in the headline or first line.
- Use as visual markers, not decoration.

**Hashtag strategy:**
- 3-5 per post on the final line only.
- Mix branded tags (#AICreativeGovernance, #CreativeAuthenticityScale) with
  discovery tags (#AIStrategy, #CreativeLeadership, #MarketingAI).
- Never put hashtags in the body text.
- Rotate discovery tags to test reach.

**Tagging:**
- Max 3 people per post.
- Only tag people mentioned in the content or whose work you are sharing.
- Do not tag for visibility. Tag for attribution.

**Line breaks:**
- Use empty lines between paragraphs.
- LinkedIn collapses multiple consecutive line breaks into one.
- One empty line between paragraphs is sufficient.

**Carousel specs:**
- PDF upload preferred over image series.
- Dimensions: 1080x1080px (square) or 1080x1350px (portrait).
- Max 20 slides per carousel.
- File size limit varies but keep under 100MB.
- Title each carousel in the post text (the PDF filename is not visible).

**Character limits:**
- Post text: up to 3,000 characters.
- First ~210 characters visible before "see more" truncation.
- Newsletter subject line: under 60 characters for full display.

## X / Twitter Formatting

**Thread numbering:**
- "1/" at the start of each tweet. Consistent format throughout.
- No variation (do not switch between "1.", "1)", "1/" mid-thread).

**Hashtag strategy:**
- 1-2 max per tweet. At the end of the tweet.
- Discovery tags only: #AI, #Creativity, #CreativeAI, #MarketingAI.
- No branded hashtags on X (lower discovery value than LinkedIn).

**Quote tweet format:**
- Add your take above the quoted tweet.
- Your commentary must stand alone without reading the quoted tweet.
- Keep your addition under 200 characters to leave room for the quote.

**Image specs:**
- Max 4 images per tweet.
- Minimum 600x335px.
- 16:9 aspect ratio displays best in feed.
- GIFs supported (auto-play in feed).

**Character limit:**
- 280 characters per tweet.
- Premium allows longer tweets, but keep to 280 for maximum reach.
- URLs consume 23 characters regardless of actual length.

**Links:**
- Put links in a reply, not in the main tweet.
- The algorithm deprioritizes tweets with external links.
- If you must include a link, put it in the final tweet of a thread.

## Repurpose Adaptation Rules

### Carousel to Thread

- Extract the core idea from each slide into a standalone tweet.
- Add transitional phrases between tweets that slides do not need.
- Tweet 1 must work as a standalone hook (slide 1's hook may need rewording
  for 280-character format).
- Drop design notes. Thread is text-only unless you attach images.

### Newsletter to Thread Excerpt

- Pull the 7-12 most interesting, self-contained points from the newsletter.
- Each point becomes one tweet. Condense heavily.
- Tweet 1 hooks the reader. Final tweet links to the full newsletter (in a reply).
- This is a teaser, not a replacement. Leave the reader wanting more.

### Text Post to Tweet

- Distill to the single strongest insight under 280 characters.
- If it does not fit in 280 characters, it becomes a thread instead.
- The hook line of the text post is usually the best candidate for the tweet.
- Drop hashtags from LinkedIn; use X-appropriate tags instead.

### Thread to Text Post

- Expand the thread into a full narrative with paragraphs.
- Add context and nuance that the 280-character limit forced you to cut.
- Combine related tweets into single paragraphs.
- Add a proper conclusion and CTA that threads often lack.

### Cross-Platform General Rules

- Never copy-paste between platforms. Each has different audience behavior.
- LinkedIn audiences expect depth and professional framing.
- X audiences expect brevity and conversational tone.
- Adjust formality: LinkedIn is boardroom, X is coffee shop.
- Timing: post LinkedIn content in the morning, X content throughout the day.
